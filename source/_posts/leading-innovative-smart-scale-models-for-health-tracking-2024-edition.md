##  What to Look For in a Smart Bathroom Scale

 Before purchasing a smart bathroom scale, you'll want to consider what you want it for. Are you embarking on a weight loss journey and want to regularly track your progress and learn about your Body Mass Index (BMI) and lean muscle mass? Or, do you just want a higher-tech scale that will provide more health metrics than your normal scale? It's important to figure out your goals, so you have a better idea of which smart scale is best for you.

 Many smart bathroom scales can sync up with [fitness trackers](https://some-techniques.techidaily.com/new-health-advocacy-through-strategic-social-media-plays/) or accompanying apps. This feature is particularly nice if you want to keep up-to-date body metrics in one place. Sometimes, you may be able to look over information from your smart bathroom scale, fitness activity stats, and overall progress from one app.

 The maximum weight limit may be another factor you want to consider. For people in larger bodies, finding a scale with a higher weight capacity may be a top priority. Some picks on our list go up to 400 pounds.

 Lastly, quite a few smart bathroom scales allow for multiple user accounts---some have enough storage for up to 16 people. Perhaps you're part of a fitness group training for a marathon, or you live with a few people who also want to use the scale. Regardless, it's an option that many items on our list offer and is something you can keep in mind.

### How do smart scales measure body fat?

 Most smart scales use bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) to measure body fat. This involves standing on small (painless) electrodes while barefoot to take the reading.

### Are smart scales worth it?

 If you're looking to track more than just your weight, investing in a smart scale is worth the price of admission. They're especially useful for folks actively monitoring their health, as they can take a wide variety of measurements.

### Are smart scales safe to use during pregnancy?

 This varies by product. Be sure to consult your physician along with the owner's manual to determine the safety of your smart scale.
