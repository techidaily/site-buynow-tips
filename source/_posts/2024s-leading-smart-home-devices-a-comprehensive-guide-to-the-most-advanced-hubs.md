##  What to Look for in a Smart Home Hub

 A smart home hub is an essential piece of kit to centralize your home's [smart devices](https://extra-support.techidaily.com/2024-approved-masterpiece-in-motion-capture-sonys-x1000-action-gear/). By connecting them all with one hub, you have control of an ecosystem where everything interacts in harmony, providing you with the effortless convenience of a fully automated smart home.

 Depending on your needs, some smart home hubs are screenless and entirely voice-controlled, while others also work as smart displays on which you can view TV, movies, photos, or live camera feeds.

 For instance, the [Amazon Echo Dot](https://www.amazon.com/All-New-release-Smart-speaker-Charcoal/dp/B09B8V1LZ3?tag=htgprimedaysite-20) is a great entry-level smart home hub that will appeal to budget buyers. Entirely voice-controlled, it has Alexa built-in and offers an extensive list of compatible devices that can be integrated into your setup via the Alexa app.

 Conversely, for larger families, something like the [Echo Show 15](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0BHZX3TYV?tag=htgprimedaysite-20) offers an exhaustive list of features to benefit any smart home setup. Thanks to its built-in camera, great for audio and visual media streaming, it can also be used with video doorbells, baby monitors, and other security devices. With customizable widgets and split-screen capabilities, it's the perfect way to stay connected to your smart devices and to each other.

 There's a different smart home hub solution for every household. Different devices will suit different types of smart home setups. Whether you're looking for the [best smart speaker](https://facebook-record-videos.techidaily.com/updated-maximizing-revenue-with-youtubes-2024-partner-program/) or the [best smart display](https://facebook-video-content.techidaily.com/new-in-2024-a-comprehensive-overview-of-multiscreen-streaming-techniques-on-social-media-platforms-like-facebook/), there's plenty to choose from.

This voice-activated home hub works with Alexa and allows for connected smart devices' voice and motion detection control.  The list of Amazon-owned and Alexa-compatible devices is also vast. It can also be used as a handy home intercom system with other Echo devices around the home.

 On the downside, and in a change from the 4th generation Echo Dot, there is no Zigbee hub integration here. You'll also notice that the previous generation's 3.5mm output port is also gone. Granted, if you didn't use these features in the past, you won't miss them here.

 With boosted audio, including customizable EQ controls via the Alexa app, and a considerable amount of Alexa-enabled smart home devices, you might feel the Echo Dot 5th Gen more than compensates for these omissions.

 Amazon Echo Dot (5th Gen) works well as an entry-level smart home hub. ### Do I Need a Hub for a Smart Home to Work?

 Smart home devices will work either with or without a smart home hub. However, if you want to operate all your smart home devices easily under one single ecosystem, then a smart home hub will give you the freedom to interconnect your devices, to enjoy simple and convenient control of everything.

### How Do Smart Home Hubs Work?

 Smart home hubs essentially connect all smart home devices in the home onto one automated network, which then controls communication between those devices. It therefore allows you to control everything from one place, either through voice commands or via the associated smartphone app.

### Do All Smart Home Hubs Work With All Smart Home Ecosystems?

 The short answer is no. Depending on the smart home hub itself, it may only be compatible with one specific smart home ecosystem. The Apple HomeKit Mini is an example of this, as it is only compatible with Apple HomeKit.
